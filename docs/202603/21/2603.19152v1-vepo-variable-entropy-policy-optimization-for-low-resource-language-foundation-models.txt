## Abstract 

Large language models frequently exhibit sub-optimal performance on low resource languages, primarily due to inefficient subword segmenta-tion and systemic training data imbalances. In this paper, we propose Variable Entropy Pol-icy Optimization (VEPO) , which leverages Re-inforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards to incorporate deterministic structural constraints into the policy alignment process. This frame-work ensures prescribed sequence length, ro-bust format consistency, and rigorous linguis-tic well formedness, all enforced during train-ing. Central to our approach is a variable en-tropy mechanism that enables the model to dy-namically calibrate the equilibrium between lit-eral fidelity and semantic naturalness by modu-lating the exploration exploitation manifold. By integrating entropy tempered advantage estima-tion with asymmetric clipping, VEPO sustains robust exploration while mitigating policy col-lapse. Empirical evaluations across 90 FLORES-200, COMET-22, chrF directions demonstrate that VEPO yields substantial improvements in both tokenization efficiency and translation qual-ity, bridging the performance gap for underrepre-sented languages.

## 2. Related Work 

Modern multilingual LLMs endeavor to harmonize high-fidelity translation with general purpose instruction follow-ing, typically through architectural specialization or so-phisticated objective blending. Apertus is developed with a primary focus on methodological transparency and exten-sive multilingual coverage—particularly for low-resource and otherwise underrepresented languages—while inten-tionally avoiding reliance on explicit, task-specific trans-lation templates (Apertus et al., 2025). TOWER+ exem-plifies a phased adaptation regime, integrating continued pre-training, supervised fine-tuning, and preference opti-mization with verifiable rewards to balance translation ac-curacy with conversational utility (Rei et al., 2025). Con-currently, EMMA-500 and LLaMAX investigate strategies for massively multilingual adaptation (Ji et al., 2025; Lu et al., 2024), while Aya explores the efficacy of instruction tuning in diverse linguistic contexts ( ¨ Ust¨ un et al., 2024).

Regional Models for ASEAN Languages. Several initia-tives focus on Southeast Asian (SEA) linguistic landscapes through curated data and targeted alignment. COMPASS-V2 targets SEA e-commerce scenarios by optimizing the synergy between curated corpora and bespoke tokenization (Maria, 2025). SEA-LION (Ng et al., 2025), Sailor2 (Dou et al., 2025), and SeaLLMs 3 (Zhang et al., 2024) extend re-gional coverage through continual pre-training and culture-aware sampling. Furthermore, Babel (Zhao et al., 2025) demonstrates how expansive multilingual scaling can ef-fectively complement localized regional efforts. 

Dedicated Machine Translation Systems. Specialized NMT models advance the state-of-the-art by prioritiz-ing bilingual fidelity through task-specific architectures. Seed-X (Cheng et al., 2025), Qwen-MT (Qwen, 2025), and Hunyuan-MT (Zheng et al., 2025) demonstrate that translation-centric designs can achieve performance parity with commercial systems. Recent compact models, such as X-ALMA (Xu et al., 2025), further optimize efficiency via modular adapters and selective rejection mechanisms, elu-cidating optimization levers that remain potent at smaller parameter scales. 

Reinforcement Learning for NMT. Policy optimization in NMT has evolved from minimum risk training, which di-rectly optimizes sequence-level metrics (Shen et al., 2016), to sophisticated preference-driven alignment. Recent work on Contrastive Preference Optimization (CPO) sharpens re-ward landscapes for translation tasks (Xu et al., 2024a), while tailored RLHF pipelines address cost-sensitive pref-erence modeling (Xu et al., 2024b; Zhu et al., 2024). Crit-ically, diagnostic studies have elucidated the fundamen-tal weaknesses of unconstrained RL objectives and eval-uation protocols (Choshen et al., 2020), prompting recent extensions into multilingual preference optimization (Dang et al., 2024). 

Limitations vs. VEPO. Despite their versatility, general-purpose LLMs often exhibit three systemic failure modes in translation: (1) Fidelity Gaps , where the absence of task-specific constraints leads to semantic hallucinations or overt translation errors; (2) Verbosity Bias , characterized by redundant supplementary explanations or conversational filler that detracts from concise output (see Figure 1); and (3) Generation Overrun , a phenomenon where the model continues generating irrelevant text or ”hallucinatory con-tinuations” after the target translation is completed. VEPO diverges from existing multilingual paradigms by address-ing these issues across three dimensions. First, unlike specialized architectures such as Qwen-MT (Qwen, 2025) that are often confined to rigid templates, VEPO main-tains expansive instruction-following capabilities while en-suring bilingual accuracy. Second, VEPO mitigates redun-dancy and over-generation through the synergy of RLVR-integrated structural constraints and length-invariant rein-forcement learning normalization, providing robust control 2VEPO: Variable Entropy Policy Optimization 

over sequence termination. Third, by introducing a variable entropy mechanism, VEPO enables the model to dynami-cally calibrate the trade-off between literal fidelity and se-mantic naturalness, facilitating both exact and paraphrastic translations beyond the limitations of traditional alignment objectives. 

## 3. Methods 

To ensure a robust foundation for effective reinforcement learning on low-resource corpora, we expand the tokenizer to mitigate subword fragmentation, followed by continued pre-training on billion-scale multilingual corpora to estab-lish a strong linguistic baseline. During the supervised fine-tuning phase, we supplement the model with high-quality bilingual translation data and instruction-following datasets covering both high-resource and underrepresented languages. Finally, we apply Variable Entropy Policy Op-timization to execute entropy-aware RL, achieving precise policy alignment while maintaining stylistic flexibility. 

Continued Pre-training 

Continued pre-training serves to adapt large language models to specialized linguistic domains while preserving general-purpose capabilities (Conneau et al., 2020a). In low-resource scenarios, the architectural design of the vo-cabulary is paramount. Introducing language-specific to-kens facilitates the capture of distinct morphological struc-tures (Conneau et al., 2020b; Fan et al., 2020; Devlin et al., 2019), thereby mitigating tokenization inefficiency through optimized subword segmentation (Sennrich et al., 2016; Liu et al., 2018). 

Stage 1: Vocabulary Expansion. We extend the Qwen2.5-7B tokenizer (Qwen et al., 2025) by integrating dedi-cated tokens for eight low-resource languages, yielding the Qwen2.5-7B-8Langs tokenizer. This expansion follows es-tablished multilingual methodologies (Team et al., 2022) to ensure robust coverage. 

Stage 2: Balanced Multilingual Training. To prevent catastrophic forgetting and maintain English proficiency, we employ a 1:1 sampling ratio between English and low-resource corpora. The expanded vocabulary introduces ap-proximately 3k–4k tokens per target language, achieving a 

2.1×–5.4× improvement in compression ratios. This ef-ficiency gain translates directly into reduced inference la-tency and enhanced computational throughput. 

Post-Training Alignment Supervised Fine-Tuning. The alignment process initi-ates with supervised fine-tuning on a 7M-sample mix-ture, incorporating a 5:1 ratio of general instructions to multilingual data. We adopt a three-phase curriculum: (i) basic instruction following, (ii) translation for South-east Asian (ASEAN) languages, and (iii) integrated trans-lation and reasoning tasks. This progression allows the model to incrementally master complex multilingual in-teractions. Preference rewards are derived from a reward model trained across ten categories, including accuracy, fluency, and code-mixing adherence (Appendix A.3).

The VEPO alignment framework incorporates five pivotal innovations to stabilize and refine multilingual policy opti-mization: 

(1) Temperature-Consistent Ratios. Unlike standard RLHF implementations that introduce distribution mis-match between sampling ( πτ ) and loss computation ( π1.0), VEPO strictly aligns the temperature τ across both stages. This preserves the martingale property essential for critic-free architectures like GRPO; we empirically observed that neglecting this consistency leads to biased gradient di-rections and premature training collapse in low-resource regimes. (2) Token-Level Contribution Balancing. In-spired by DAPO (Yu et al., 2025), we employ token-level normalization to ensure that samples of varying lengths contribute proportionally to the gradient. This mecha-nism effectively suppresses the verbosity bias inherent in sequence-level objectives, which is critical for maintaining concise and accurate translations. (3) Position-Aware En-tropy Scheduling. We introduce a dynamic entropy mul-tiplier (1 + αHγ t) that decays with output length. This ensures a high-entropy exploration space in initial decod-ing stages to capture diverse linguistic mappings, while gradually enforcing lower entropy to prioritize literal fi-delity and structural precision as the sequence progresses. 

(4) Constraint-Driven Trajectory Filtering. Integrated with the RLVR module, we implement a rigorous filter-ing strategy to prune rollout samples that exhibit linguistic 3VEPO: Variable Entropy Policy Optimization 

pathologies, such as intra-sentence code mixing, sequence length redundancy, or ”off-target” language drift. This selective optimization ensures that the policy aligns ex-clusively with high-quality, constraint-compliant trajecto-ries, thereby enhancing output robustness in sensitive low-resource domains. (5) Communication-Efficient Advan-tage Normalization. Following the REINFORCE, we nor-malize advantages using the group-wise mean but compute the standard deviation at the micro-batch level. This design choice significantly reduces inter-node synchronization and collective communication bandwidth overhead, enabling high-throughput distributed training while maintaining suf-ficient gradient stability for actor-only updates.

The core philosophy of the VEPO is to sustain a high en-tropy exploration space within a controlled range through-out the reinforcement learning process. Unlike conven-tional alignment objectives that often lead to premature mode collapse resulting in monotonous or overly literal outputs. Our approach encourages the model to maintain a rich probability distribution across potential candidate to-kens. This high entropy state is particularly crucial for mod-els that already possess strong multilingual foundations; it provides the necessary stochasticity to move beyond rigid structural mappings and explore more nuanced, paraphras-tic expressions. Under the refined guidance of the Reward Model, the policy is optimized to maximize rewards not only for semantic accuracy but also for stylistic elegance. Consequently, VEPO facilitates the emergence of ”liberal translation” capabilities, allowing the model to generate translations that are both contextually accurate and linguis-tically sophisticated, effectively capturing the underlying intent rather than merely performing surface-level word substitution.

There-fore, entropy regularization is not merely “uncertainty”: it increases the support coverage over the paraphrastic equiv-alence class, enabling the policy to represent and discover diverse yet faithful translations. 

5.2. Entropy-Regularized Optimization Geometry 

In token level policies, mode collapse corresponds to the policy approaching the boundary of the probability sim-plex, where exploration vanishes and optimization be-comes ill conditioned. For a categorical policy with prob-abilities p, the Fisher information takes the form G =diag( p) − pp ⊤, whose eigenvalues shrink toward 0 as p

becomes deterministic. The entropy bonus βH (πθ ) coun-teracts this boundary attraction by penalizing degenerate distributions, maintaining a non-trivial probability floor for paraphrastic alternatives and improving numerical condi-tioning. Position-dependent exploration. Paraphrastic divergence in translation predominantly happens early in decoding, when the model selects a lexicalized phrase or idiomatic template. We thus apply a position-decayed coefficient γt to concentrate exploration on early tokens, while allowing later tokens to collapse toward a coher-ent realization of the chosen paraphrastic template. This yields a controlled exploration–commitment dynamic that increases paraphrastic coverage without inducing late-stage drift.

## 6. Conclusion 

In this paper, we introduced Variable Entropy Policy Op-timization , a comprehensive framework for adapting foun-dation models to low-resource linguistic environments. Our approach systematically addresses the primary bottle-necks in multilingual modeling through targeted tokenizer expansion, balanced continued pre-training, and entropy-aware reinforcement learning. By integrating Reinforce-ment Learning with Verifiable Rewards, we enforce deter-ministic structural constraints directly within the optimiza-tion loop, effectively mitigating common failure modes such as sequence inflation and markup corruption. Empir-ical results across 90 FLORES-200, COMET-22 and chrF directions demonstrate that VEPO achieves state-of-the-art translation performance while preserving robust general-purpose capabilities. Future research will focus on refining reward models for high-fidelity paraphrastic evaluation and exploring ad-vanced RL methodologies to better handle linguistic diver-sity. The principles of dynamic entropy modulation and verifiable alignment presented here offer a promising foun-dation for building more robust, inclusive, and expressive multilingual models.
